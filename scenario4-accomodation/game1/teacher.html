Checking Into Accommodation

Full script with teaching notes

📋 Index 👩 Student 👩‍🏫 Teacher 📝 Vocabulary

🎓 Before You Start — Teacher Notes

  • 📌 Level: Beginner to Pre-Intermediate
  • 📌 Context: Vietnamese woman, new meat worker, checking into share house
  • 📌 Grammar Focus: Polite questions with "Can I...?"
  • 📌 Common Error: "I book a room" → Correct to "I booked a room" (past tense)
  • 📌 Cultural Note: Explain Australian slang — G'day, No worries, Mate
  • 📌 Role Play Tip: Teacher reads Dave's lines, student reads Mai's lines

Full Conversation Script

👨 Dave (Landlord)
*(opens door)*
"G'day! Can I help you, love?"
👩 Mai
*(knocks on door)*
"Hello? Excuse me... I am Mai. I book a room here?"
👨 Dave (Landlord)
"Oh yes! You must be from the agency.
What's your full name, mate?"
👩 Mai
"Yes, my name is Mai Nguyen. N-G-U-Y-E-N.
I start work at the meatworks on Monday."
👨 Dave (Landlord)
"Lovely! Welcome, Mai. Come on in —
I'll show you around."
👩 Mai
"Thank you. Can I please see my room?"
👨 Dave (Landlord)
"Sure thing! Your room is down the hall, second door on the left.
The bathroom is shared — two other workers use it too.
Here's your key."
👩 Mai
"Is there a washing machine I can use?"
👨 Dave (Landlord)
"Yep! It's out the back, near the laundry.
You can use it any time before 9pm.
Don't use it after that — the neighbours complain."
👩 Mai
"What time must I be quiet at night?
My work starts very early."
👨 Dave (Landlord)
"Quiet time is 10pm. Most workers here start early too,
so everyone goes to bed early. You'll fit right in!"
👩 Mai
"I understand. Thank you very much, Dave.
I am happy to stay here."
👨 Dave (Landlord)
"No worries, Mai! If you need anything,
just knock on my door. Make yourself at home!"

Teaching Focus Points

📚 Grammar & Pronunciation

  • 🔤 "I booked" — Past tense of book. Students often say "I book" — correct this.
  • 🔤 "Can I please...?" — Very polite. Teach the stress: "Can I PLEase..."
  • 🔤 "Must I...?" — Formal question. Also teach "Do I have to...?"
  • 🔤 Spelling names: Practice spelling out loud. "N-G-U-Y-E-N"

🦘 Australian Slang in This Lesson

Dave Says Standard English Notes
"G'day!""Hello!"Most common Australian greeting
"Love" / "Mate""Friend"Friendly, not romantic
"Sure thing!""Yes, of course!"Very common
"Out the back""Behind the house"Directional phrase
"No worries!""You're welcome / It's OK"Most common Australian phrase
"Make yourself at home""Feel comfortable here"Welcoming phrase
"You'll fit right in!""You will belong here easily"Encouragement

⭐ Extension Activity

  • Ask students to role play again — swap roles (student plays Dave!)
  • Ask: "What other questions would you ask your landlord?"
  • Practice: "Can I cook fish in the kitchen?" / "Where do I put my rubbish?"
  • Write a text message from Mai to her family back home in Vietnam about her new room
