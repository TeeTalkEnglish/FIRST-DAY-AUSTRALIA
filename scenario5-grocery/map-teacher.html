Teaching Idea

1

Ask the student to choose one product, for example milk or bread.

2

The student asks you: Where is the milk section?

3

You answer with directions only. Do not say the section name if you want it harder.

4

The student drags the section card to the map and checks the answer.

📚 Teaching Tips

📍 Location Words

Key phrases for giving store directions:

"at the back wall", "at the front", "on the left wall", "on the right side", "in aisle 3", "near the entrance", "next to", "opposite", "between"

🗣️ Model Questions

Encourage polite question forms:

"Excuse me, where can I find the milk?"
"Could you tell me where the bread is?"
"Which aisle is the Vegemite in?"

👂 Listening Focus

Tell students to listen for:

Position: back wall, front, left, right
Aisle: aisle 1–5, top/back shelf, bottom/front shelf
Relative: next to, opposite, between, near

🇦🇺 Aussie Vocab

Slip in Aussie words while giving directions:

"The snags are in meat" (sausages)
"Spuds are in produce" (potatoes)
"The lollies are in aisle 3" (candy)

🔄 Repeat & Confirm

Have the student repeat back:

Teacher: "The dairy is on the back wall, next to the bakery."
Student: "So... the dairy is on the back wall?"
Teacher: "That's right!"

⬆ Extension

After completing the map:

1. Student gives directions to YOU (role swap)
2. "I need ___, which aisle?"
3. Plan a shopping route
