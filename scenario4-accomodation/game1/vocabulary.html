Key words, useful phrases and Australian slang

Key Vocabulary

Word Meaning Example
Accommodation A place to sleep and live "I need accommodation near the meatworks."
Landlord The person who owns the house "My landlord is named Dave."
Check in Arrive and register at your place "I need to check in today."
Shared bathroom Bathroom used by many people "The bathroom is shared."
Laundry Room with a washing machine "The laundry is out the back."
Quiet time Hours when you must not make noise "Quiet time starts at 10pm."
Agency Company that found your job/house "I came from the meatworks agency."
Meatworks Factory where meat is processed "I start at the meatworks Monday."
Share house House where many people live together "I live in a share house with workers."

Useful Phrases

🚪 When You Arrive

  • "Hello, I have a booking. My name is Mai."
  • "I am here to check in."
  • "I booked a room through the agency."
  • "I start work at the meatworks on Monday."

❓ Asking Questions

  • "Can I see my room, please?"
  • "Where is the bathroom?"
  • "Is there a washing machine?"
  • "What time is quiet time?"
  • "Can I cook in the kitchen?"
  • "Where do I put my rubbish?"
  • "Is there Wi-Fi?"

🙏 Being Polite

  • "Excuse me..."
  • "Thank you very much."
  • "I understand."
  • "Sorry, can you say that again?"
  • "I am happy to stay here."

🥩 About Your Work in Australia

🗺️ Common Work Locations

Many Vietnamese workers come to Australia on a 482 visa to work in abattoirs (meat processing factories).

Common regional locations include:

🗺️ Queensland — Rockhampton, Townsville, Mackay
🗺️ Victoria — Shepparton, Mildura, Ballarat
🗺️ New South Wales — Wagga Wagga, Tamworth
🗺️ South Australia — Murray Bridge, Naracoorte

🔪 Useful English at the Meatworks

  • "I am a meat boner / slicer / butcher."
  • "I work the early shift."
  • "What time does my shift start?"
  • "Where do I collect my uniform?"
  • "Can you show me how to do this?"
