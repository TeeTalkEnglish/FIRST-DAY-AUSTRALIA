🎭 How to Play Your Role

1
Welcome Lan: “Hi! Need a SIM today?”
2
Ask about where she’ll live & data needs. Listen carefully.
3
Present Optus Flex Plus first. Emphasise the bigger data for only $20.
4
If she worries about country coverage, compare Telstra’s 8 GB for $25.
5
Negotiate. If she wants cheaper, offer it for \$30 → lowest \$28.
6
Close: activate SIM, give receipt, wish her luck at the meat plant.

💳 Explain Plan Types to the Student

The student may not know the difference between Prepaid and Upfront (Postpaid). Use the cards below to explain. Remember — Lan can only get Prepaid because she just arrived and has no Australian ID or bank account.

✅

Prepaid

Sell This to Lan

💵 Pay first, use later
📅 28 or 30 day cycle
🪪 No ID or credit check needed
📝 No contract — flexible
🛒 Available at your shop right now
🔄 Student just recharges when it expires
❌

Upfront (Postpaid)

Not Available for Lan

💳 Use first, get a bill each month
📋 Locked 12–24 month contract
🪪 Requires 100 points of Australian ID
🏦 Requires Australian bank account
📱 May include a phone (added to bill)
⚠️ Early cancellation fee applies

🎭 How to Explain This During the Role-play:

"Since you just arrived, prepaid is the way to go — no ID needed."
"Upfront plans need Australian ID and a bank account. You can switch to one later when you're settled."
"With prepaid you just pay now and you're good for 28 days. Easy!"
"When your 28 days finish, you can recharge online or come back to the shop."
🤫
If the student asks about Upfront plans: Explain politely that they need 100 points of ID (passport = 70 points, so they still need a driver's licence or bank statement). Steer them back to prepaid. This is realistic — most new arrivals cannot pass a postpaid credit check.
