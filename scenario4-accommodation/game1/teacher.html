Checking Into Accommodation

Read Dave's lines out loud. Wait for Kim to respond!

📋 Index 👩 Student 👩‍🏫 Teacher 📝 Vocabulary

🎓 Quick Teacher Notes

  • 📌 Level:  Beginner to Pre-Intermediate
  • 🎯 Grammar Focus:  Polite questions — "Can I please...?" / "Is there a...?" / "How much is...?"
  • ⚠️ Common Error:  "I book" → correct to "I booked" (past tense)
  • 🦘 Slang to explain:  G'day · No worries · Mate · Sure thing · Out the back · Fortnight
  • 💡 Tip:  Speak slowly and naturally. Pause after each question so Kim has time to think.
  • 🎭 Acting tip:  Be friendly and welcoming like a real Australian landlord! Smile and use gestures.
🎯 Progress Step 1 of 12
1

Open the door

Step 1 of 12

🎭 Your Line — Read this out loud!
*(opens door and smiles)* "G'day! Can I help you, love?"
💬 Say "G'day" like a friendly Australian! "Love" is a friendly word — not romantic. Open the door wide and smile 😊
👩 Kim should say...

She should greet you, say her name (Kim) and tell you she has a booking for a room.
Listen for: "Hello... I am Kim... I book a room here?"

📝 Teaching Moment If Kim says "I book a room" — that is okay for now. After the game, teach her: "I booked a room" — past tense is correct!
2

Ask for her full name

Step 2 of 12

🎭 Your Line — Read this out loud!
"Oh yes! You must be from the agency. Welcome!
What's your full name, mate?"
💬 Say "mate" naturally — it just means friend. Sound warm and welcoming! Pause clearly after "What's your full name?" so Kim knows to answer.
👩 Kim should say...

She should say her full name — Kim Huynh.
She should spell her family name: H — U — Y — N — H.
She should say when she starts work — Monday at the meatworks.
Listen for all three parts!

📝 Teaching Moment If Kim's spelling is unclear — ask her to repeat: "Sorry, can you spell that again slowly?" Spelling your name is very important in Australia!
3

Welcome her inside

Step 3 of 12

🎭 Your Line — Read this out loud!
*(gestures inside)* "Lovely! Welcome, Kim. Come on in —
I'll show you around the house."
💬 Wave your hand to invite Kim inside. "Lovely!" = That's great! "Come on in" = Please come inside. Say it warmly and step back to let her in.
👩 Kim should say...

She should say thank you and politely ask to see her room.
She should use polite language: "Can I please...?"
Listen for: "Thank you... Can I please see my room?"

📝 Teaching Moment "Can I please...?" is very polite English. If she says "Can I see room?" — help her add "please" and "my". Articles and politeness are very important!
4

Show the room and bathroom

Step 4 of 12

🎭 Your Line — Read this out loud!
*(walking down the hall)* "Sure thing! Your room is down the hall here —
second door on the left. Here's your key!
The bathroom is just next door — it's shared with two other workers.
You can use it any time from 5am onwards."
💬 Point and gesture as you speak! "Sure thing" = Yes of course. "Down the hall" = further along the corridor. Hand Kim an imaginary key 😄 Speak slowly — there is a lot of information!
👩 Kim should say...

She should ask about the shared bathroom — how many people use it and what time she can use it in the morning.
She should mention she starts work very early.
Listen for: "How many people...?" "What time can I...?"

📝 Teaching Moment Teach Kim: "How many people share the bathroom?" — this is a very natural question. Also: "Is it okay if I use it at 4:30am?" — teach her to be specific with times!
5

Explain bathroom details

Step 5 of 12

🎭 Your Line — Read this out loud!
"Just two other workers — Tom and Sarah.
They both start at 7am so you'll have the bathroom to yourself in the early morning!
Just make sure you leave it clean after you use it, yeah?"
💬 "Yeah?" at the end = Do you understand? It is very casual Australian English. Say it with a friendly nod. This is reassuring good news for Kim!
👩 Kim should say...

She should ask about the washing machine.
Is there one? Where is it? Is it free to use?
Listen for: "Is there a washing machine...?" "Is it free?"

📝 Teaching Moment Teach the difference: "Is there a...?" = asking if something exists. "Where is the...?" = asking location. Kim should try to use both!
6

Explain the washing machine

Step 6 of 12

🎭 Your Line — Read this out loud!
*(walking to the back of the house)* "Yep! It's out the back — just through that door there.
It's free to use — but only before 9pm please.
The neighbours next door complain if it runs late at night.
There's also a clothesline outside to hang your washing."
💬 "Out the back" = behind the house. Point towards the back door. Say "the neighbours complain" with a serious but funny face! 😄 Pause so Kim understands the 9pm rule.
👩 Kim should say...

She should ask about the kitchen — can she cook? Is there a fridge? Can she store her food?
She might mention she cooks Vietnamese food.
Listen for: "Can I use the kitchen?" "Is there a fridge?"

📝 Teaching Moment Teach Kim: "Can I keep my food in the fridge?" — this is very useful. Also: "Is it okay if I cook fish and strong-smelling food?" — very important to ask in a share house!
7

Explain the kitchen

Step 7 of 12

🎭 Your Line — Read this out loud!
*(showing the kitchen)* "Of course! The kitchen is shared by everyone.
You have your own shelf in the fridge — the bottom one is yours.
Please label your food with your name, yeah?
And make sure you clean up after yourself — leave the kitchen tidy for the next person!"
💬 Point to the fridge and show her the bottom shelf. "Label your food" = write your name on it. Say "clean up after yourself" clearly — this is a very important Australian phrase!
👩 Kim should say...

She should ask about Wi-Fi internet.
Is there Wi-Fi? What is the password?
She might explain she needs to call her family in Vietnam.
Listen for: "Is there Wi-Fi?" "What is the password?"

📝 Teaching Moment Teach Kim the pronunciation: "Why-Fy" — many students say it differently. Also teach: "Is the Wi-Fi included in the rent?" — a very practical question!
8

Give her the Wi-Fi details

Step 8 of 12

🎭 Your Line — Read this out loud!
"No worries! Yes, Wi-Fi is included in the rent.
The network name is RiversideHouse and the password is...
Sunshine2024 — capital S, all one word.
I'll write it down for you — it can be a bit tricky!"
💬 "No worries" = You're welcome / That's fine. Spell out the password slowly: "S-U-N-S-H-I-N-E two-zero-two-four". Pretend to write it on a piece of paper and hand it to Kim!
👩 Kim should say...

She should ask about the rubbish bins — where do they go?
And ask about cleaning the shared areas — who is responsible?
Is there a cleaning roster?
Listen for: "Where do I put the rubbish?" "Who cleans...?"

📝 Teaching Moment Teach the word "roster" = a schedule showing whose turn it is. Also teach: "rubbish bin" (Australian English) vs "trash can" (American English). Use Australian words!
9

Explain rubbish and cleaning

Step 9 of 12

🎭 Your Line — Read this out loud!
*(showing the cleaning roster on the fridge)* "Good question! The rubbish bins are outside near the back gate.
Bin day is every Tuesday morning — you need to put the bins out the night before.
And here's the cleaning roster on the fridge — everyone takes turns cleaning the kitchen and bathroom.
You're on cleaning duty every second Saturday!"
💬 Point to an imaginary roster on the fridge. "Bin day" = the day rubbish is collected. "Every second Saturday" = one Saturday on, one Saturday off. Say "You're on cleaning duty!" with a smile 😄
👩 Kim should say...

She should ask about quiet time at night.
She should explain her work starts at 5am and she leaves very early.
She should ask if it's okay to move around quietly in the morning.
Listen for: "What time must I be quiet?" "I start at 5am..."

📝 Teaching Moment Teach Kim: "I will try not to disturb anyone." This shows respect for housemates — very important in Australian share houses! Also teach: "Is it okay if I...?" for asking permission politely.
10

Explain quiet time rules

Step 10 of 12

🎭 Your Line — Read this out loud!
"No problems at all! Quiet time is from 10pm to 7am.
Most workers here start early so everyone understands.
Just try to be quiet when you leave — close doors softly, no loud TV or music.
You'll fit right in here, Kim — everyone is very friendly!"
💬 Say the times clearly: "TEN pm to SEVEN am". Mime closing a door softly. "You'll fit right in" = you will belong here easily. Say it warmly to make Kim feel welcome! 😊
👩 Kim should say...

She should ask about paying the rent.
How much per week?
When does she pay — weekly or fortnightly?
How does she pay — cash or bank transfer?
Listen for: "How much is the rent?" "When do I pay?" "How do I pay?"

📝 Teaching Moment Teach the word "fortnight" = two weeks. This is very common in Australia! Also teach: "bank transfer" vs "cash" — most Australian landlords prefer bank transfer.
11

Explain rent and payment

Step 11 of 12

🎭 Your Line — Read this out loud!
"The rent is \$210 per week — that includes all the bills and Wi-Fi.
I prefer bank transfer — every Monday morning before 9am please.
The bond is two weeks rent — so \$420 — you pay that today.
I'll give you a receipt for everything, no worries!"
💬 Say the numbers clearly: "TWO hundred and TEN dollars" and "FOUR hundred and TWENTY dollars". Teach Kim: "bond" = a security deposit you get back when you leave. "Receipt" = written proof of payment.
👩 Kim should say...

She should ask what to do if there is a problem or emergency.
What if something breaks in her room?
Who does she call at night or on weekends?
She should ask for Dave's phone number.
Listen for: "What do I do if...?" "Can I have your number?"

📝 Teaching Moment Teach Kim: "What do I do if there is an emergency?" This is a very useful phrase in daily life in Australia. Also: "Can I please have your phone number?" — practice the polite form with "please"!
12

Give your number — Final Step! 🎉

Step 12 of 12

🎭 Your Line — Read this out loud!
*(writing phone number down)* "Of course! My number is 0412 783 556 — I'll write that down for you.
Call me any time if something breaks or you need help, okay?
If it's an emergency at night, don't hesitate — just call!
I think you're going to love it here, Kim. Make yourself at home!"
💬 Read the phone number digit by digit slowly: "zero four one two — seven eight three — five five six". "Don't hesitate" = don't be afraid or shy. "Make yourself at home" = feel comfortable here. End with a big warm smile! 😊
👩 Kim should say...

This is her final response — make sure she says a lot!
She should say she understands everything.
She should say thank you warmly.
She should say she is very happy to live here.
She might say she is excited for work and will be a good tenant!
Listen for a long, warm response with lots of good English!

📝 Final Teaching Moment After Kim finishes — give her positive feedback! Tell her: "Well done! Your English was great today." Point out 2-3 things she did very well, then 1-2 things to practice more. Always end positively! 🌟
🎓

Lesson Complete!

Kim has finished the full 12-step check-in conversation!
She covered: name, room, bathroom, kitchen, Wi-Fi, rubbish, quiet time, rent and emergencies.
Excellent lesson! 🌟
